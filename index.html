Mechanics of Materials

What is Mechanics of Materials?

Mechanics of Materials is the study of how materials deform and fail under various loads, such as tension, compression, torsion, or bending. It analyzes stresses and strains within structures like beams, columns, and pressure vessels to ensure they can withstand applied forces safely. Key concepts include material properties, deformation, and failure theories, vital for designing reliable engineering components like bridges, machines, and welds.

1. Stress and Strain

Normal Stress: \( \sigma = \frac{F}{A} \) - Force per unit area, axial or bending.

Shear Stress: \( \tau = \frac{V}{A} \) - Shear force over area, e.g., in bolts or beams.

Axial Strain: \( \epsilon = \frac{\delta}{L} \) - Change in length over original length.

Shear Strain: \( \gamma \approx \theta \) - Angular deformation in radians.

Hooke's Law: \( \sigma = E \epsilon \) - Linear stress-strain relation, \( E \) is Young's modulus.

Poisson's Ratio: \( \nu = -\frac{\epsilon_{\text{lateral}}}{\epsilon_{\text{axial}}} \) - Lateral to axial strain ratio.

Shear Modulus: \( G = \frac{E}{2(1 + \nu)} \) - Relates shear stress to shear strain.

2. Axial Loading

Deformation: \( \delta = \frac{F L}{A E} \) - Axial elongation or compression.

Thermal Deformation: \( \delta_T = \alpha L \Delta T \) - Expansion due to temperature change, \( \alpha \) is thermal coefficient.

Total Deformation: \( \delta = \sum \frac{F_i L_i}{A_i E_i} + \alpha L \Delta T \) - Combines mechanical and thermal effects.

Statically Indeterminate: Use equilibrium (\( \sum F = 0 \)) and compatibility (deformations match).

3. Torsion

Shear Stress (Circular): \( \tau = \frac{T r}{J} \), \( J = \frac{\pi r^4}{2} \) - Max stress at outer radius.

Angle of Twist: \( \phi = \frac{T L}{G J} \) - Rotational deformation of shaft.

Power: \( P = T \omega \) - Transmitted by rotating shaft, \( \omega \) in rad/s.

Non-Circular: Use torsion constant \( K \), \( \tau_{\text{max}} = \frac{T}{K} \).

4. Bending

Flexural Stress: \( \sigma = \frac{M y}{I} \) - Bending stress, max at farthest distance \( y \).

Moment of Inertia (Rectangle): \( I = \frac{b h^3}{12} \) - Resistance to bending.

Shear Stress: \( \tau = \frac{V Q}{I t} \) - Varies across beam cross-section, \( Q \) is first moment.

Max Shear Stress (Rectangle): \( \tau_{\text{max}} = \frac{3 V}{2 b h} \) - At neutral axis.

5. Beam Deflection

Elastic Curve: \( \frac{d^2 v}{dx^2} = \frac{M}{E I} \) - Relates moment to deflection.

Point Load (Simply Supported): \( \delta_{\text{max}} = \frac{P L^3}{48 E I} \) - Max deflection at center.

Uniform Load (Cantilever): \( \delta_{\text{max}} = \frac{w L^4}{8 E I} \) - Max at free end.

Superposition: Add deflections from multiple loads.

6. Combined Loading

Principal Stresses: \( \sigma_{1,2} = \frac{\sigma_x + \sigma_y}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left( \frac{\sigma_x - \sigma_y}{2} \right)^2 + \tau_{xy}^2} \) - Max/min normal stresses.

Max Shear Stress: \( \tau_{\text{max}} = \sqrt{\left( \frac{\sigma_x - \sigma_y}{2} \right)^2 + \tau_{xy}^2} \) - At 45Â° to principal planes.

Mohr's Circle: Center at \( \left( \frac{\sigma_x + \sigma_y}{2}, 0 \right) \), radius \( R = \sqrt{\left( \frac{\sigma_x - \sigma_y}{2} \right)^2 + \tau_{xy}^2} \).

7. Columns and Buckling

Euler's Buckling Load: \( P_{cr} = \frac{\pi^2 E I}{L_e^2} \) - Critical load for slender columns.

Effective Length: \( L_e = K L \), \( K \) depends on end conditions (e.g., 1 for pinned-pinned).

Slenderness Ratio: \( \lambda = \frac{L_e}{r} \), \( r = \sqrt{\frac{I}{A}} \).

8. Failure Theories

Maximum Normal Stress: \( \sigma_1 \leq S_y \) - For brittle materials.

Maximum Shear Stress (Tresca): \( \tau_{\text{max}} \leq \frac{S_y}{2} \) - For ductile materials.

von Mises Stress: \( \sigma' = \sqrt{\sigma_1^2 + \sigma_2^2 - \sigma_1 \sigma_2} \leq S_y \) - For complex loading.

Factor of Safety: \( n = \frac{S_y}{\sigma_{\text{max}}} \).

9. Thin-Walled Pressure Vessels

Hoop Stress: \( \sigma_h = \frac{P r}{t} \) - Circumferential stress.

Longitudinal Stress: \( \sigma_l = \frac{P r}{2 t} \) - Axial stress.

Spherical Vessel: \( \sigma = \frac{P r}{2 t} \).

10. Thick-Walled Pressure Vessels

Radial Stress (Cylindrical): \( \sigma_r = \frac{P_i r_i^2 - P_o r_o^2 - r_i^2 r_o^2 (P_i - P_o) / r^2}{r_o^2 - r_i^2} \).

Hoop Stress (Cylindrical): \( \sigma_\theta = \frac{P_i r_i^2 + P_o r_o^2 + r_i^2 r_o^2 (P_i - P_o) / r^2}{r_o^2 - r_i^2} \).

Longitudinal Stress (Cylindrical): \( \sigma_z = \frac{P_i r_i^2}{r_o^2 - r_i^2} \).

Spherical Vessel: \( \sigma_\theta = \sigma_\phi = \frac{P_i r_i^3 + P_o r_o^3 + 2 r_i^3 r_o^3 (P_i - P_o) / r^3}{2 (r_o^3 - r_i^3)} \).

11. Fatigue Design

S-N Curve: \( S_f = a N^b \) - Stress vs. cycles to failure.

Endurance Limit: \( S_e \approx 0.5 S_u \) - Max stress for infinite life (steel).

Goodman Diagram: \( \frac{\sigma_a}{S_e} + \frac{\sigma_m}{S_u} = \frac{1}{n} \) - Accounts for mean and alternating stresses.

Fatigue for Welds: Use AISC/ASM standards for allowable stress.

Fatigue for Shafts: \( S_e' = k_a k_b k_c k_d S_e \) - Adjust for surface, size, load, temperature.

12. Energy Methods

Strain Energy: \( U = \frac{1}{2} \sigma \epsilon V \) - Stored elastic energy.

Castigliano's Theorem: \( \delta_i = \frac{\partial U}{\partial P_i} \) - Deflection from energy derivative.

Virtual Work: \( \delta = \int \frac{M m}{E I} dx \) - Deflection using virtual moments.
